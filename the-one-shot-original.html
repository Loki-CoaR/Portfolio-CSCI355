The One Shot Original

January 2026 – June 2026

About

The One Shot Original is a simple campaign that I came up using the existing world's structures and reimagined them such that you can spin a story in any direction you choose given a subset of the available characters in the narrative in a compact 28 episodic series.

Key Items.

Key Items are a special category of permanent items that players can obtain only once in-game. They serve various important purposes such as unlocking new mechanics or streamlining/altering existing ones. A Key Item's use may either be automatic or require manual interaction. (Unless it leaves your inventory and is retrieved/returned). Examining a key Item COULD result in another discovery.

Consumable Items.

Consumables can help the Fools better deal with all kinds of danger they might come across in the journey. They can buff, debuff, cleanse, inflict, and deal damage depending on their text. Careful! All consumables have a item durability of ONE and because of this- CAN ONLY BE USED ONCE.

Readable Items.

Readable Items are collectible items which can be obtained from Dungeons or Quests. They feature stories or reveal history and lore for your reading pleasure. I know you wont read them. Some Readable Items are time-gated and can only be obtained if the previous part was already obtained. For example, Letter from the Dean 1 and Letter from the Dean 2. You could not read the 2nd without having read the 1st.

Currency.

Currency is the primary economic resource in the game. It can be obtained through various means such as completing quests, selling items, or defeating enemies. Every world has its own unique currency system. Every time period has its own unique currency system. Be mindful of this as you go around and trade with Merchants in Towns.

Quests.

Quests are objectives that players can undertake to progress the story and earn rewards. They can be main story quests or side quests, each with their own unique challenges and outcomes.

Dungeons.

Dungeons are challenging areas that players can explore to find treasures, defeat powerful enemies, and uncover hidden secrets. Each dungeon has its own unique layout, enemies, and rewards.

Repositioning.

Repositioning allows you to change your character's position within battle. This can be useful for strategic reasons or to access different areas.

Navigation.

You can navigate the world via your map! You'd be cooked without one! Simply point to your destination and It will pin your objective~ automatically revealing the location and the estimated length of time before your arrival. Careful not to accumulate TOO much Exhaustion on your travels, the journey won't do you good if you drop dead. If you wish to travel somewhere else, simply remove your pin or reset your map.

Towns.

Towns are safe havens where players can rest, trade, and interact with non-player characters. They serve as central hubs for the game world, offering various services and information. There are stores in every town. Merchants can be found in every town, ready to buy and sell items taking your available currency.

Achievements.

Through the basis of sorrow, happiness can be achieved.

Bonds.

Maintaining relations are important, you know. No one will shed a tear if die by your lonesome.

Sanity.

Sanity is a measure of your character's mental stability. It can be affected by various events and choices throughout the game. Low Sanity can lead to negative effects and gameplay consequences. If your Sanity reaches zero, your character breaks into psychosis.

MAX HP/MP.

HP (Health Points) represent your character's physical well-being. MP (Magic Potential) represents your character's magical energy. Both are crucial for survival and spellcasting. If your HP reaches zero, your character is defeated. If your MP reaches zero, it will not replenish until long rest.

Stats.

STR, DEF, AGL, WIS, MIND, CHAR.

  • STR determines how much dmg you can dish out.
  • DEF acts as an additional hp bar. It absorbs damage before your HP is affected.
  • AGL determines turn order. If you are double somoeone's SPD, you will go twice.
  • CHAR affects your character's social interactions with other people, low speaking skills = No friends.
  • WIS influences your character's observation skills and knowledge-based assumptions.
  • MIND affects your character's resistance to mental effects, magic damage, and affects your Magic Output.

    Shock (Lightning)

    Status Debuff
    • Using Lightning attacks will immediately deal Lightning DMG and occasionally apply the Shock status. (Read the text before assuming you dummy)
    • Shock will cause enemies to take Lightning DoT at the start of the turn. This DOT dmg scales with your Effect Mastery.
    • Ranking up the Shock Status will inflict a debuff on your opponent that could force them to lose their turn.

    Infected -> Diseased -> Overcome.

    Less Common Statuses
    • Sickness is a slow transformation. To Cure Infected, You must make a blood sacrifice.
    • Your conditions worsen. To Cure Diseased, Someone else must administer healing.
    • You are beyond saving. There is nothing that can be done for you at this point.

    Frostbite -> Hypothermia -> Frozen

    Status Debuff
    • Using Ice attacks will immediately deal Frost DMG and occasionally apply the Frozen status. (Read the text before assuming you dummy)
    • Freeze will cause enemies to be unable to take their turns and take Additional Ice DMG when they dethaw.
    • Shattering an enemy prematurely from the 3 Turn Freeze Duration will inflict the enemy with additional SHATTER Dmg.

    Burn -> Ablaze -> Immolated.

    Status Debuff
    • Using Fire attacks will immediately deal Burn DMG and occasionally apply the Burn status. (Read the text before assuming you dummy)
    • You are on fire and will take dmg every turn. The first applicaiton of BURN will reduce STR by 50%.
    • You can spread the fire dmg, this varies with each tier.

    Bruised -> Mangled -> Broken.

    Status Debuff
    • This status effect is mostly given by blunt weapons or certain items. (Read the text before assuming you dummy)
    • Bruised makes you take more damage from all sources.
    • Mangled lowers your item usage to 1 item per turn as well as still taking double damage.
    • Broken makes it so you cant do anything :] make sure you get outside help.

    Poison -> Acid -> Toxic.

    Status Debuff
    • Poison will increase the difficulty of performing repositions.
    • Acid increases that difficulty as well as damaging you each turn.
    • Toxic will make your Max hp go down temporarily until fixed each turn. (Think of Pokemon)

    Wet -> Soaked -> Drenched.

    Status Debuff
    • Wet reduces your MAX SANITY each turn.
    • Soaked adds a chance of dropping a held item.
    • Drenched reduces your Max Sanity each turn by double with the increased chance of dropping items even if your not holding them.
    • The likelihood of being inflicted with SHOCK increases with each tier.

    Ensnared.

    Status Debuff
    • You're stuck and may not declare any attacks.
    • You can still declare non attacking moves though.
    • To recover from the ensnared status ailment, There is a 1/2 chance you break free from your attempt.
    • If unsuccessful, the second attempt will have a guaranteed 100% chance of escape.

    Cursed.

    Status Debuff
    • At the start of the turn, take 1 damage.
    • At the beginning of each turn, there is a 50% chance of warding off the curse.
    • If unsuccessful in warding off the curse, it will stay until the curse is removed or HP -> 0.

    Drowsy -> Asleep.

    Status Debuff
    • If the drowsy character declares any action, the turn afterwords - they will be put to sleep.
    • While you're sleeping, you heal your HP by +2 every turn.
    • Characters inflicted with Asleep cannot take any actions until they wake up.

    Haste/Hindered/Exhausted/Knocked Down.

    Less Common Statuses
    • Haste: +1 Move.
    • Hindered: -1 Move.
    • Exhausted: Lose a turn.
    • Knocked Down: Cannot take actions until they stand up.

    Smitten/Enraged.

    Less Common Statuses
    • Foes who are smitten in love have a heightened chance of missing when attacking against their beloved.
    • While under the influence of rage, you will be locked into your basic attacks. STR stat is doubled, while DEF and evasion are halved.
